Key Findings

  • Honorlock pricing ($8-15/exam) is among the highest in the market. Per-exam pricing may be costing more than necessary for high-volume institutions.
  • Respondus LockDown Browser + Monitor offers the best value for institutions already using compatible LMS platforms, with site licensing that eliminates per-exam fees.
  • All AI-based proctoring systems show documented bias against students with darker skin tones, disabilities, and non-standard testing environments.
  • Accessibility compliance varies widely - many vendor VPATs are outdated (2+ years old) and should be independently verified.
  • Student anxiety and complaints are significant with all proctoring systems, but vary in severity based on surveillance intensity.
  • Safe Exam Browser offers an open-source alternative for basic lockdown functionality without privacy concerns, but lacks robust proctoring features.

4 Accessibility Compliance

Critical analysis of accessibility standards compliance across all evaluated systems.

Critical Research Finding: Bias in AI Proctoring

A 2022 Frontiers in Education study found significant disparities in automated proctoring software:

  • Students with darker skin tones were significantly more likely to be flagged for review
  • Black students showed increased likelihood of being marked for potential cheating
  • Women with darkest skin tones were far more likely to be flagged than any other group
  • Root cause: Training datasets predominantly composed of white males aged 18-35

Source: Frontiers in Education - Racial, skin tone, and sex disparities in automated proctoring software

      Extended Time Accommodations

      All vendors support extended time, but implementation varies. Verify accommodation workflows with your Disability Services office.

    • !
      Assistive Technology Compatibility

      Browser lockdown features may conflict with screen readers, magnification software, and other assistive technologies. Test thoroughly before deployment.

      AI Flagging and Disabilities

      Students with movement disorders, tics, or visual impairments may be disproportionately flagged by AI algorithms that detect "suspicious" behavior.

    7 Recommendations

    Based on our comprehensive analysis, here are our recommendations for the Ed Tech Committee.

    Primary Recommendation: Respondus LockDown Browser + Monitor

    For institutions seeking the best balance of cost, functionality, and reliability, Respondus offers the strongest value proposition. The site licensing model eliminates per-exam cost anxiety and provides predictable budgeting. With 2,000+ institutions already using the platform, it represents a proven, stable solution.

    Visit Respondus Learn About Monitor

    Alternative Considerations

    Proctorio - Best for Maximum Flexibility

    If instructor control and configuration options are paramount, Proctorio offers the most granular settings. However, weigh this against documented privacy controversies and student concerns.

    ProctorU - Best for High-Stakes Exams

    For certification programs or high-stakes assessments where live proctoring is required, ProctorU's Live+ tier provides the strongest integrity guarantee, albeit at premium pricing.

    Safe Exam Browser - Best for Privacy-Conscious

    For institutions prioritizing student privacy or seeking a supplementary lockdown tool, SEB offers a free, open-source option. Best used alongside other assessment integrity measures.

    Recommended Next Steps

    1. Request current VPATs from top vendor candidates (ensure they're less than 2 years old)
    2. Schedule demos with Respondus and one alternative (Proctorio or Examity)
    3. Conduct pilot testing with Disability Services to verify accessibility workflows
    4. Survey faculty about current Honorlock pain points and feature priorities
    5. Negotiate pricing based on actual exam volume and multi-year commitments
    6. Review contracts for FERPA compliance, data retention policies, and exit clauses
